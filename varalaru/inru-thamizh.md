# இன்று தமிழ் | Tamil as People Use It Today

The history files trace how the language got here; this one records how it *lives* now — who speaks what, where, in which register, and which rooms of life Tamil currently keeps or has surrendered. For phase 2 this is the most operational file in `varalaru/`: **evolution that doesn't land in the registers people actually live in changes a museum, not a language.**

## 1. எங்கே, யார் | Where and Who

~80+ million speakers. Official: Tamil Nadu & Puducherry, Sri Lanka, Singapore; taught school-language in Malaysia. Diaspora in three layers, each with different Tamil: colonial-era indenture communities (Malaysia, South Africa, Fiji, Caribbean — heritage varieties, some shifted); the post-1983 Eelam exodus (Canada — Toronto holds one of the largest Tamil populations outside Asia — UK, Europe, Australia: first generation fully fluent, literature-producing; second generation heritage-speaking); and the professional migration wave (Gulf, US tech corridors). Sri Lankan Tamil (Jaffna, Batticaloa) is famously **conservative** — closer in several features to older Tamil than Tamil Nadu speech is; the periphery preserved what the center innovated away.

## 2. இரண்டு தமிழ் | The Two Tamils, Lived Daily

The diglossia (எ-003) is not academic; it is every speaker's daily commute:

- **எழுத்துவழக்கு** (written register): news reading, textbooks, formal speech, this repo's Tamil. Grammar-book forms: வருகிறேன், இல்லை, அது.
- **பேச்சுவழக்கு** (spoken register): home, street, feeling. வர்றேன், இல்ல, அது→அதுவா/அதான்.

Nobody speaks written Tamil at dinner; almost nobody wrote spoken Tamil until cinema dialogue, fiction, and the internet started to. Children effectively acquire one Tamil at home and are handed a second at school — Ilam's own profile (fluent speech, script being built) is this national condition in one person. The two registers are far enough apart that machine systems trained on web text inherit the split (see சோதனை-002 §6).

## 3. பல தமிழ் | The Many Spoken Tamils

Region: Chennai (with its street-mixed *Madras bashai*), Kongu, Madurai, Nellai, Jaffna, Batticaloa, Malaysian/Singapore Tamil — mutually intelligible, proudly distinct. Community: sociolinguistics documents caste-correlated varieties (lexicon and morphology differences — classic studies contrast e.g. ஜலம்/தண்ணீர் household vocabularies); naming this is necessary precisely for the bias ledger: **which variety got to be "standard" was a power outcome, not a linguistic one.** Broadcast and print standardized on a formal register no community speaks natively — the one "neutral" Tamil is the one that is nobody's mother tongue.

## 4. தங்கிலிசு | Tanglish and the Roman Keyboard

The live code of young Tamil Nadu: Tamil matrix + English embeds, and in writing, **Tamil in Roman letters** — chat, comments, captions ("epdi irukka? seri seri"). Documented as strongest among urban youth (~17–22); the 2011 *Why This Kolaveri Di* moment made it globally visible. Input tools for Tamil script are now excellent, but the romanized habit persists — typing follows the path of least friction, and the friction history (early encoding chaos, English keyboards) shaped a generation's thumbs. Two honest readings, both true: Tanglish is register-loss in progress *and* it is the living proof that Tamil speakers instinctively engineer hybrid media to say what they mean fastest. The instinct is an asset; its current direction drains the script.

## 5. மேடை, திரை, ஒலி | Platform, Screen, Feed

Three institutions set today's registers. **மேடைத்தமிழ்** — the Dravidian movement's platform oratory: alliterative, rhythmic, crowd-tuned; political Tamil is still performed, not just spoken. **Cinema** — the true register-setter: dialogue put spoken varieties on screen and made them legitimate; lyricists (கண்ணதாசன் → வைரமுத்து → the current generation) function as the era's public poets; a film line can install a phrase in 70 million mouths in a week — **no phase-2 coinage channel is remotely as powerful.** **The feed** — YouTube, podcasts, reels: for the first time in history, *spoken* Tamil is being mass-recorded and archived at scale; the register that was never written now has a corpus growing daily.

## 6. இழந்த அறைகள், காத்த அறைகள் | Rooms Lost, Rooms Held

**Held:** home and kinship, politics, cinema/music, religious life, literature (vibrant — contemporary fiction, சிற்றிதழ் little-magazine culture, and a powerful Eelam/diaspora literature born of the war), primary education.
**Lost or leased out:** higher education in STEM (overwhelmingly English; Tamil-medium engineering exists but uptake is thin), medicine, corporate work, much of the courts, software. This is **domain starvation**: a language stays fully alive only if whole domains of thought happen in it. Tamil's technical registers are starving not for capacity (கணினி-era coinage proved capacity) but for *use* — the words exist; the rooms speak English. Logged as எ-008.

## 7. கணினியுள் தமிழ் | Tamil in the Machine

Unicode settled the encoding wars; fonts and input are mature; Tamil web presence grows but remains modest relative to 80M speakers (Tamil Wikipedia is on the order of 1.5–2 lakh articles — hedged, mid-2020s figures — versus English's millions). The NLP/LLM ecosystem treats Tamil as a *relatively* low-resource language despite its size: corpora skew formal (the diglossia again — the web wrote எழுத்துவழக்கு), technical text is thin (domain starvation again), and tokenizer economics tax the script (measured in `../aaivu/sothanai-002-alavu.md`). Efforts like AI4Bharat's Indic models and tokenizers are closing the gap. The structural verdict from the experiment: **Tamil's machine-era handicaps are contingent — corpus and infrastructure — not grammatical.**

## 8. எண்ணத்திற்கு | What Today's Map Means for Phase 2

Three operational conclusions. **Ship in the living register:** a coinage that exists only in எழுத்துவழக்கு is a museum exhibit; the cinema/feed channel is where words actually install. **The spoken corpus is being born right now** — the feed is archiving பேச்சுவழக்கு at scale for the first time; whatever we build should ride that, not fight it. **The hybrid instinct (Tanglish) is the user-demand signal:** speakers are already engineering faster media daily; phase 2's job is to give that instinct targets inside Tamil rather than out of it.
