# பாடம் 02 · சொல்: கோர்க்கும் மொழி
# Lesson 02 · Words: The Language That Threads Beads

Prerequisite: பாடம் 01 drills in progress. This lesson stays simple; everything here deepens later.

## 1. The one big idea: words are built, not memorized

Tamil is **agglutinative** — "gluing." A word is a root plus suffixes threaded on like beads, each bead one meaning, in a fixed order, nothing hidden:

> வீடு (house)
> வீடு + கள் → வீடுகள் (houses)
> வீடு + இல் → வீட்டில் (in the house)
> வீடு + இலிருந்து → வீட்டிலிருந்து (from within the house)
> வீடுகளிலிருந்தும் (from the houses, too) — house + plural + from-within + also

English changes words unpredictably (go/went, mouse/mice). Tamil almost never does — it *adds*, and every addition keeps its shape and its meaning. You already do this in speech without knowing its name. The name is the whole lesson: **சொல் = வேர் + விகுதிகள்** (word = root + suffixes), assembled like beads, readable like beads.

## 2. Four beads to start

Only four case-beads now (there are eight; the rest come in பாடம் 04):

| Bead | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| -ஐ | (object marker) | வீட்டை பார் — see *the house* |
| -இல் | in/at | வீட்டில் — in the house |
| -க்கு | to/for | வீட்டுக்கு — to the house |
| -ஓடு / -உடன் | with | அவனோடு — with him |

And the plural bead **-கள்** always threads *before* the case bead: வீடு-கள்-இல் → வீடுகளில்.

## 3. Why did வீடு become வீட்டு?

You noticed வீடு + இல் became வீட்**ட்**டில், not வீடில். When beads meet, their edges adjust — sounds double, slide, or bridge. This system is called **புணர்ச்சி** (joining). It follows rules, not whims, and gets its own lesson (பாடம் 05). For now, only *notice* the joins; your speech already knows most of them. Trust the ear, note the surprises in the journal.

## 4. இரு திணை | The two "kinds" — meet the grammar's worldview

Every Tamil noun belongs to one of two classes:

- **உயர்திணை** "high-kind": humans, deities
- **அஃறிணை** "non-high-kind": everything else — animals, things, ideas

The class controls verb endings and question words (யார்? for people, என்ன? for the rest). You cannot speak a sentence without silently classing its subject. Note the value baked into the names themselves — *high* and *not-high*: a worldview inside the grammar. Flagged in the research track (எ-004, and `../varalaru/arivu-varalaru.md` §10); learn the mechanics now, question them later — both are this project.

## 5. பயிற்சி | Drills

1. **Bead ladder.** Take 5 roots you use daily (வீடு, கை, ஊர், மரம், நண்பன்). Build 4 forms each with the beads above — write, then say aloud. 20 words from 5.
2. **Unthreading.** Take 10 long words you *say* naturally (வீட்டுக்குப்போறேன்…). Write them, then split into beads. Where you can't split, journal it — data.
3. **திணை sort.** List 20 nouns; class each உயர்திணை / அஃறிணை. Find one that feels wrong to class (a pet? a god's statue? an AI?). Bring it to the next session — that discomfort is a research finding.

## 6. நூல் | Closing text — குறள் 391

> கற்க கசடறக் கற்பவை கற்றபின்
> நிற்க அதற்குத் தக.

*Learn flawlessly what is worth learning; then stand by what you learned.* Full reading in `../ilakkiyam/thirukkural.md`. Bead-practice: find the root கல் (learn) appearing three times in different dress — கற்க, கற்பவை, கற்றபின்.

## 7. ஆய்வுக்கு | Fed to the research track

Bead-transparency means **meaning stays decomposable** — a Tamil word can be unpacked without residue, which is exactly the "lossless" property phase 2 needs (→ `../aaivu/saaram.md` §1). The திணை classification is the counter-finding: a *forced judgment* in every sentence (எ-004). One lesson, one gift, one bias — both recorded.
