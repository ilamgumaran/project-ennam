# பாடம் 01 · எழுத்து: உயிரும் மெய்யும்
# Lesson 01 · The Script: Soul and Body

The Tamil script is not an arbitrary inventory — it is a small theory of how form carries life, encoded in its own names. This lesson gives the whole system as one picture; the drills that follow live inside it.

## 1. உயிர் எழுத்து — the soul letters (12)

உயிர் means *life, soul*. The vowels are the letters that sound on their own:

> அ ஆ இ ஈ உ ஊ எ ஏ ஐ ஒ ஓ ஔ

Each comes short or long — **குறில்** (kuril, short) and **நெடில்** (nedil, long):

| குறில் | அ | இ | உ | எ | ஒ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **நெடில்** | ஆ | ஈ | ஊ | ஏ | ஓ |

ஐ and ஔ count as நெடில். You already make every one of these distinctions in speech (பல் *tooth* / பால் *milk*); the work is only binding them to marks.

## 2. மெய் எழுத்து — the body letters (18)

மெய் means *body* (and also *truth*). The consonants are bodies without breath — written with the dot, the **புள்ளி**, and unable to sound alone:

> க் ங் ச் ஞ் ட் ண் த் ந் ப் ம் ய் ர் ல் வ் ழ் ள் ற் ன்

The tradition sorts them into three இனம் (classes):

| இனம் | எழுத்துகள் | Character |
|---|---|---|
| வல்லினம் (hard) | க ச ட த ப ற | stops — the percussion |
| மெல்லினம் (soft) | ங ஞ ண ந ம ன | nasals — the hum |
| இடையினம் (medium) | ய ர ல வ ழ ள | glides and liquids — the flow |

Built into the traditional ordering is real phonology: each hard letter is paired with the nasal made at the same place in the mouth — க்–ங், ச்–ஞ், ட்–ண், த்–ந், ப்–ம், ற்–ன். Say each pair and feel that the tongue does not move. The alphabet's order is itself a map of the mouth.

## 3. ஆய்தம் — the odd one (1)

**ஃ** — three dots, the ஆய்த எழுத்து. Rare, ancient, neither soul nor body: அஃது, எஃகு. Note it and move on.

## 4. உயிர்மெய் — soul enters body (216)

A body letter takes a soul letter and becomes a living syllable:

> க் + அ = க க் + ஆ = கா க் + இ = கி …

18 bodies × 12 souls = **216** உயிர்மெய். With 12 + 18 + 1, the full count is **247** — not memorized as a number but generated by the system: (soul) + (body) + (soul-in-body) + (the odd one).

The souls appear inside combined letters as signs:

> ா ி ீ ு ூ ெ ே ை ொ ோ ௌ (and அ lives inside the bare letter: க)

**Where fluent speakers actually stumble:** the உ/ஊ combinations, which change shape by letter rather than by one rule — கு சு டு து பு று but கூ சூ டூ தூ பூ றூ, each its own form. This is the one region of the script that needs plain drilling; it is drill #3 below.

Loanword letters (கிரந்தம்: ஜ ஷ ஸ ஹ க்ஷ ஸ்ரீ) sit outside the 247 — recognize them; they need no drilling now.

## 5. பயிற்சி | Drills

1. **Read aloud, daily, small.** One column of உயிர்மெய் a day (all of க-row: க கா கி கீ கு கூ கெ கே கை கொ கோ கௌ), sounding each; speech does the checking that a beginner's eye cannot.
2. **Write from sound.** Take ten words you say every day; write them before checking. Errors go in the journal — they are data (முறை §3).
3. **உ/ஊ forms.** Write the உ and ஊ row for all 18 consonants until the irregular shapes stop needing thought.
4. **Pair check.** Say the six hard–nasal pairs (க்–ங் …) noting the unmoved tongue.

## 6. நூல் | Closing text — திருக்குறள் 1

> **அகர முதல எழுத்தெல்லாம் ஆதி**
> **பகவன் முதற்றே உலகு.**

*As அ is first among all letters, so the Primal is first of the world.* The tradition's very first couplet is about the first letter — script and metaphysics joined in the opening breath of its most famous book. Read it letter by letter; find the உயிர், the மெய், the உயிர்மெய்.

## 7. ஆய்வுக்கு | Fed to the research track

The naming itself — உயிர் soul, மெய் body/truth, உயிர்மெய் soul-in-body — is a theory of expression embedded in an alphabet: form is a body that only lives when breath enters it. Logged as seed **வி-002**. Whether other scripts carry comparable self-theory is an open question for phase 2.
